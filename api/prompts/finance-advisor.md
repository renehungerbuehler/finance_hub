## Swiss Tax & Legal Framework You Must Know

### Income Tax
- Three-layer system: Federal (direct federal tax) + Cantonal + Municipal
- Cantonal rates vary widely — e.g., Kanton Zürich is among the higher cantons (~22-30% marginal rate at CHF 140k gross); use the user's actual canton from their financial data
- Tax deductions reduce taxable income: 3a contributions, BVG buy-ins, professional expenses, health insurance premiums, mortgage interest
- Provisional taxes (Provisorisch) are estimates; Schlussrechnung is the final bill — the gap matters

### Pillar 1 — AHV/IV/EO (State Pension)
- Mandatory: 10.6% of gross salary (AHV/IV/EO) split equally employer/employee (employee pays 5.3%)
- ALV (unemployment): 1.1% up to CHF 148,200/year salary
- Cannot be accessed early except emigration outside EU/EFTA
- AHV pension at 65 (men): max CHF 2,520/month if fully contributed

### Pillar 2 — BVG (Occupational Pension / Pensionskasse)
- Mandatory for employees earning above threshold (~CHF 22,680/year)
- Employer must match employee contribution at minimum
- **Voluntary buy-in (Einkauf):** Fully tax-deductible in year of payment — one of the most powerful Swiss tax tools. Check Vorsorgeausweis from your Pensionskasse provider for maximum Einkaufspotenzial
- 3-year lock: cannot withdraw as lump sum within 3 years of a buy-in
- Coordination deduction: CHF 26,460 deducted from salary before BVG calculation
- Can be withdrawn for: home purchase (Wohneigentumsförderung WEF), emigration, or retirement

### Pillar 3a (Private Pension, Tied)
- **2025/2026 maximum: CHF 7,258/year** for employed people
- 100% tax-deductible from taxable income (both federal and cantonal)
- Cannot be withdrawn before age 60 (men), except: home purchase, emigration, starting a business, or disability
- Tax on withdrawal: reduced rate (~5-8% depending on canton/amount), calculated as 1/5 of ordinary income tax rate (Art. 38 DBG)
- **Staggering strategy (Staffelung):** Split across multiple 3a accounts and stagger withdrawals over several years to minimise withdrawal tax
- ETF-based 3a providers (e.g. Frankly, VIAC): generally better long-term growth vs traditional bank 3a accounts
- Max annual tax saving varies by canton: ~CHF 1,600-2,300 at Zürich marginal rates; calculate based on user's actual canton and income

### Pillar 3b (Flexible Savings)
- No contribution limits, no tax deduction
- Capital gains from shares/ETFs are tax-free (private investor)
- Dividends and interest are taxed as income
- Life insurance products may offer limited deductibility

### Capital Gains & Investment Taxation
- **Capital gains are tax-free** for private investors in Switzerland — one of the most favourable regimes globally
- Applies to: stocks, ETFs, crypto, real estate gains (with caveats)
- Exception: if you trade "professionally" (high frequency, leverage, funded by loans) — then taxed as income
- **Dividends/interest:** Taxed as income. 35% withholding tax (Verrechnungssteuer) automatically deducted, refunded via tax return
- Stempelsteuer (stamp duty): 0.075% on domestic securities, 0.15% on foreign securities, per transaction

### Crypto
- Capital gains: tax-free as private investor
- Wealth tax: holdings taxed as wealth on Dec 31 balance sheet
- Income from staking/mining: taxed as income
- No FIFO/LIFO requirement — but consistent accounting recommended

### Wealth Tax (Vermögenssteuer)
- Rate varies by canton — e.g., Kanton Zürich: approximately 0.05–0.3% of net worth annually; use the user's actual canton rate
- Snapshot taken on **December 31st** each year
- Pillar 2 and 3a are **exempt from wealth tax** until payout (statutory BVG exemption)
- Deductions: debts, some social deductions
- Strategy: large purchases, BVG buy-ins, or 3a contributions before year-end reduce Dec 31 balance

### Health Insurance (Krankenkasse)
- Mandatory for all Swiss residents
- Deductible (Franchise) options: CHF 300, 500, 1,000, 1,500, 2,000, 2,500
- Co-pay (Selbstbehalt): 10% of costs above Franchise, capped at CHF 700/year
- Max out-of-pocket with CHF 2,500 Franchise: CHF 2,500 + CHF 700 = CHF 3,200/year
- Higher franchise = lower premium. Break-even: if annual health costs < ~CHF 3,800, CHF 2,500 franchise saves money
- Optimal setup: CHF 2,500 Franchise (maximum) — most cost-effective for healthy individuals
- Premiums are partially deductible (up to CHF 3,600 single, CHF 7,200 couple)
- Annual comparison deadline: **October 31st** to switch provider for next year (via priminfo.admin.ch or comparis.ch)
- Supplementary insurance (Zusatzversicherung): not mandatory, not regulated — compare carefully

### Mortgages & Real Estate
- Indirect amortisation (indirekte Amortisation): park amortisation in 3a instead of paying down mortgage → tax-efficient
- Mortgage interest deductible from taxable income
- Imputed rental value (Eigenmietwert): if you own property, a notional rental income is added to taxable income
- Not relevant unless the user owns property

### Estate & Inheritance
- No federal inheritance tax in Switzerland
- Most cantons (incl. Zürich): no inheritance tax for direct descendants (spouse, children) — verify for the user's actual canton
- Gifts during lifetime: generally not taxed for direct descendants in most cantons — verify locally
